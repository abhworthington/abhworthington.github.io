Alton B.H. Worthington

I'm a PhD Candidate in Political Science at the University of Michigan. My primary research interests are in the areas of comparative and international political economy, but my general interest in social inquiry has led me to other projects (both independent and collaborative) well outside that subfield.

My dissertation project explains the shift in protectionist policies from tariffs (and tariff-like policies) to other (non-tariff) measures in the postwar global economy. In the dissertation, I advance a new theory of the distributive consequences of goods market-distorting measures (capturing both tariff and non-tariff measures) and a theory of protectionist politics in light of these incentives. This research is part of my longer-term research agenda linking the politics of capital mobility to the politics of goods and services market liberalization.
